E-Vote National Platform System Guide

Understand what each page does and why it exists in the platform.

This page explains the purpose of the major screens in the E-Vote National Platform so users, party representatives, and administrators can understand how the full election workflow is organized.

Users

Citizens and party members

Admins

Review and approval flow

Goal

Transparent digital voting

Platform Purpose

The system connects registration, approval, publishing, and voting in one place.

Public users can learn about the platform, sign in, and review election information.
Party representatives can submit contest requests and receive approval tokens.
Administrators verify submissions before parties become available for public voting.

Home Page

Main Dashboard

The home page is the central entry point after login. Its purpose is to guide users through the platform, present important election-related information, and provide access to public resources and next actions.

Election Page

Login and Entry

The election page currently works as the sign-in page. Its purpose is to allow registered users to authenticate and enter the platform securely before accessing protected election functions.

Sign Up Page

Account Registration

The sign-up page is used to create a new user account. Its purpose is to onboard new participants so they can later sign in, interact with the system, and take part in election-related workflows.

Student Page

Sample Directory

The student page presents profile cards in a clean responsive layout. Its purpose is to display structured profile information in a readable way and can also serve as a design reference for future directory-style pages.

Competition Request

Party Submission Form

This page allows a party representative to submit party identity, leadership details, documents, and descriptions. Its purpose is to collect all information required before a party can be reviewed for election participation.

Admin Panel

Review Center

The admin panel lists submitted party requests for staff users. Its purpose is to give administrators a controlled space to inspect requests and decide whether they should move forward in the process.

Request Approval Page

Decision Stage

This page shows the detailed request information to administrators. Its purpose is to support a careful approval decision before an approval token is generated for the requesting party.

Token Page

Approval Confirmation

The token page displays the generated approval token for the authorized user. Its purpose is to securely confirm that a request was approved and provide the controlled token needed in the next step of the workflow.

User Token Page

User Access to Token

This page allows the approved user to view their active token. Its purpose is to connect the approval process with the user-facing side of the platform in a secure and traceable way.

Party Publish Page

Public Availability

The party publish page shows approved parties that are ready for public visibility. Its purpose is to make verified party profiles available before they appear in the voting stage.

Vote Page

Party Selection

The vote page displays the list of published and approved parties. Its purpose is to let users review eligible choices before continuing to the final vote review and submission experience.

Review Vote Page

Final Check Before Voting

The review vote page focuses on a single party record. Its purpose is to give voters a final chance to inspect the selected party details before the vote is submitted through the API.

Overall Mission

Why this platform exists

The E-Vote National Platform is designed to support a structured digital election process. Its purpose is to help manage identity, party participation, approval flow, publication, and final voting in a more transparent, organized, and efficient way than a disconnected manual system.
